We are All in this Together

The world houses more than 7.7 Billion people

constantly growing without concern of natural resources or available space.

Limited Access to resources, man-made wars, and limiting reproduction are a few ways that

the human population is already limiting this supposed unlimited growth.

Don't Go Breaking My Heart

One of the biggest ways we can see if a population is increasing or decreasing is by viewing its fertility rate.

This value is measured by the average of children each woman has in their life time.

In the graphic above, we can see how the increased use of contraceptives can lower the average fertility of individual countries.

In countries where contraceptives are less prevalent, fertility rates peaks at just under 8 births per woman.

On the opposite end of the spectrum, there are countries where 96% of the population uses some form of contraceptives,

and their fertility rates are drastically lower because of it.

Besides proving that contraceptives work, this graphic also allows us to see its effect on fertility rates.
